Bookshelf

Recently, I have sparked a passion for something I used to see no value in: reading. Reading fiction novels allows me to traverse and explore new worlds — expanding the limits of my imagination — and transpose them onto the one we call reality. Reading non-fiction novels not only further educate me in topics I am passionate about, but ignites interests in new topics as well.

Below is a compiled list of books that I have read starting from 2019. Books that had a large impact on my thinking and/or inspired me are bolded.

2019
  • Neil Martinez-Belkin - The Autobiography of Gucci Mane
  • Phil Knight - Shoe Dog
  • Philip K. Dick - Do Androids Dream of Electric Sheep
  • Robert T. Kiyosaki - Rich Dad Poor Dad
  • George Orwell - Animal Farm
2020

  • E.M. Forster - The Machine Stops
  • George Orwell - 1984
  • Douglas Adams - The Hitchhiker’s Guide to Galaxy Trilogy

  • Jeffrey Kluger - Apollo 8: The Thrilling Story of the First Mission to the Moon
  • Yuval Noah Harari - Homo Deus: A Brief History of Tomorrow
